Get Involved: Literacy in Brazil

Take Action: Support Brazilian Literacy Programs

Literacy is a critical component of social and economic empowerment. In Brazil, many organizations are working to improve **foundational literacy skills** for children and adults, especially in disadvantaged communities. Getting involved can mean contributing your time, skills, or financial resources to these crucial educational efforts.

Ways to Get Involved:

  • **Volunteer:** Organizations often need volunteers for teaching, administrative support, fundraising, and community outreach.
  • **Donate:** Financial contributions help provide books, training for teachers, classroom materials, and support for students.
  • **Partnerships:** Schools, community organizations, and businesses can partner with literacy initiatives to implement programs.
LITERACY CHALLENGES

Only about one in four Brazilians is considered fully literate, and the country ranks low globally in reading skills. Literacy initiatives often focus on adult and youth education, as well as early childhood education.

NGOs & Direct Programs

Organizations focused on direct teaching and training, often for youth and adults:

  • **Instituto IBRAEMA:** Offers a Youth and Adult Literacy Program based on the Frank Laubach method, including facilitator training and free materials. Visit Site
  • **Programa Brasil Alfabetizado (PBA):** A federal government youth and adult literacy policy.
  • **Alfabetização Solidária (AlfaSol):** A program focused on reducing adult illiteracy, supported by various partners.
Foundational & Systemic Impact

Groups working on high-level educational policy, scale, and teacher support:

  • **Lemann Foundation:** Supports public education policies, including efforts to eradicate illiteracy and promote high-quality learning. Visit Site
  • **Brazil Literacy Collaborative (BLC):** A collaborative effort aiming to significantly increase literacy rates among first and second-grade public school students.
  • **Motriz (formerly Instituto Gesto):** Works to adapt and implement foundational skills programs like "Teaching at the Right Level" (TaRL) in public school systems.
International Involvement & Volunteers

Opportunities for international volunteers and fundraising:

  • **BOOKS4BRAZIL (Holistica Foundation Inc.):** Runs a read-a-thon program to raise funds and awareness for literacy efforts in Brazil. Visit Site
  • **BrazilLink:** A Portuguese language school that offers volunteer work opportunities, including teaching and fundraising projects in poor communities. Visit Site
  • **Caminhos Language Centre:** Offers short-term and long-term volunteer opportunities in Rio de Janeiro's communities, which can include reading stories and helping with children. Visit Site
