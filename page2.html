Brazil: Literacy, Poverty, and Education

CAUSE: Poverty & Food Insecurity

Over 32 million children in Brazil live in poverty. Many lack adequate food and resources, which leads to poor nutrition and missed school days.

EFFECT: Low Literacy & Attendance

Malnourished children struggle to focus and attend class. In 2021, only 43% of second-grade students were considered literate.

SOLUTION: Food & Cash Programs

Programs like Fome Zero and Bolsa Família link food and financial aid to school attendance, improving nutrition and learning outcomes.

CAUSE: Unequal School Access

Rural and poorer regions, especially in the North and Northeast, have fewer teachers and resources, leading to wide learning gaps.

EFFECT: Regional Learning Gaps

Students in these regions perform well below the national average, with higher dropout rates and lower reading levels.

SOLUTION: Teacher Training & Reform

The Sobral/Ceará model improved literacy through teacher training, tracking progress, and prioritizing early reading skills.

CAUSE: Adult Illiteracy

Many adults cannot read or write well, limiting job opportunities and making it harder to support their children’s education.

EFFECT: Intergenerational Poverty

Poor literacy keeps families trapped in poverty and limits education across generations.

SOLUTION: Adult Literacy Programs

Programs like Alfabetização Solidária help adults learn to read and write, breaking the cycle of illiteracy in families.
